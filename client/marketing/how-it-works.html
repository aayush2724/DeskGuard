How it works

Sense. Process. Display.
That's the whole stack.

DeskGuard reads the radio signals already bouncing around your building and turns them into a live, privacy-safe map of every seat. No cameras, no badges, no app for occupants.

01

Sense

Passive BLE and WiFi probes detect device presence at each desk zone. No app required for occupants.

02

Process

Our edge model fuses signals, applies a ghost-seat classifier, and emits a per-desk status update every 30 seconds.

03

Display

A 3D floor plan updates live on any display, web browser, or kiosk. Staff see a management dashboard with time-series data.

The details

What's actually happening
under the hood

Sensor placement

One low-power sensor unit covers roughly 8–12 desks depending on room geometry. Units mount discreetly under desks or on ceiling rails — no cabling to each desk required, just power and a mesh network link.

Signal fusion

BLE RSSI, WiFi probe requests, and ultrasonic distance readings are fused on-device using a lightweight model trained across 40+ deployments, so it adapts to your room's furniture and wall materials within the first week.

Ghost-seat classifier

A desk showing a device but no human presence signal for longer than your configured window (15 minutes by default) is flagged amber. This is the feature institutions ask for most — bags and laptops shouldn't reserve seats indefinitely.

Edge processing

All raw signal data is processed locally. Only the final per-desk status (free / occupied / ghost) is sent to the dashboard — hashed, anonymised, and with no device identifiers retained past the processing window.

30-second cadence

Status updates publish every 30 seconds over a lightweight WebSocket feed, fast enough to feel live on a kiosk display without flooding your network or draining sensor batteries.

Self-calibration

When you rearrange furniture or add desks, the system re-calibrates automatically over 24–48 hours rather than requiring a manual remap — just walk the new layout once with the calibration app.

Installation

Live in a week,
not a quarter

Most institutions go from signed contract to a working floor map in 5–7 business days.

Day 1–2: Survey & plan

Our team walks your space, marks sensor placement on your floor plan, and confirms power/network access points. Most rooms need zero new cabling.

Day 3–4: Install & calibrate

Sensors are mounted and joined to the mesh network. A technician walks the room to calibrate the ghost-seat classifier against your actual furniture.

Day 5: Dashboard setup

We configure your floor map, alert channels (Slack, Teams, email), and ghost-seat policy window with your facilities team.

Day 6–7: Go live

The live map goes up on your kiosk, embedded site, or app. We monitor accuracy for the first week and tune thresholds as needed.
