Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Our approach

DeskGuard is built around a simple principle: we should be able to tell you whether a desk is free without knowing or caring who is — or was — sitting at it. This policy explains what data our sensors collect, how it's processed, and what reaches our servers.

What our sensors detect

DeskGuard sensor units passively listen for BLE advertisements and WiFi probe requests emitted by nearby devices, along with ultrasonic distance readings. These are background signals that most devices broadcast automatically, and no pairing, app, or action from occupants is required.

What's processed on-device

All raw signal data is processed locally on the sensor unit. Device identifiers (such as MAC addresses) are hashed using a rotating daily salt and used only to distinguish "same device as a moment ago" for short-term continuity. These hashes are never transmitted in a form that allows tracking a device over time or linking it to an individual.

What reaches the dashboard (GDPR)

Only an aggregate, anonymous per-desk status — free, occupied, or ghost — is sent from sensor units to the DeskGuard dashboard. No device identifiers, signal strength values, or individual-level data leave the building. This means DeskGuard does not process personal data as defined under GDPR in its core occupancy feed, as no information relates to an identified or identifiable natural person.

Where institutions connect booking integrations (LibCal, Outlook, Google Calendar), reservation data may include names or email addresses depending on how the institution's calendar is configured — this data is governed by a separate data processing agreement available on request.

Data retention

Aggregate desk-status history is retained according to your plan (30 days for Starter, 1 year for Campus, custom for Enterprise) for analytics purposes. Raw sensor signal data is never retained — it's processed and discarded on-device within seconds.

Account data

We retain standard account information (names, work emails, billing details) for staff users of the DeskGuard dashboard, handled under our standard data processing terms. This is separate from the occupancy-sensing data described above.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to privacy@deskguard.io.
